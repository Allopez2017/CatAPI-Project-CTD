Quick Cat Facts

Known as Felis catus in Latin, the domestic house cat is said to have been first domesticated around 7500 BC. Cats are small carnivorous mammals that have remarkable hunting qualities inherited from their Big cat ancestors. These cute, cuddly creatures not only have sharp, retractable claws, but they also have night vision which helps them locate and catch prey.

While you might be familiar with a cat's 'Meow,' kitties actually have multiple different sounds they omit including: trilling, hissing, growling, and purring. Some believe kitties evolved to 'Meow' due to their domestication as a way to communicate with their human owners. Trilling is linked to a cat's hunting instinct. They often trill to imitate a bird call so their prey will let their guard down for them to hunt.
